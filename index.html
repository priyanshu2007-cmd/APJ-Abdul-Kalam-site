Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India:

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, fondly remembered as the "Missile Man of India" and the "People's President," was a man who rose from humble beginnings to become one of India's most beloved leaders. His life is a beacon of inspiration, reflecting values of hard work, humility, integrity, and patriotism

Early Life and Education:

orn on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam came from a modest background. His father was a boat owner, and despite financial constraints, Kalam pursued his education with determination. He graduated in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT). From an early age, he was fascinated by flight and dreams of touching the sky.

A Pioneer in Science and Technology:

Dr. Kalam began his career at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). At ISRO, he played a key role in developing the SLV-III, India's first satellite launch vehicle, which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in orbit in 1980. He earned the title "Missile Man of India" for his significant contributions to India's missile development programs, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles. His leadership in India's nuclear tests in 1998 at Pokhran marked a historic moment, making India a nuclear power.

President of the People:

In 2002, Dr. Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India. His presidency was marked by simplicity, accessibility, and a deep connection with the youth of the nation. He was not just a ceremonial head of state but a true visionary who inspired millions with his speeches, ideas, and vision for a developed India. He always emphasized the importance of education, innovation, and a strong moral foundation. His interactions with students and his belief that “dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action” left a lasting impact.

A Visionary and a Teacher:

Even after his presidency, Dr. Kalam continued to engage with students and academic institutions. He wrote several books, including the famous "Wings of Fire," an autobiography that charts his journey from a small town to the highest office in India. His other books like "Ignited Minds", "India 2020", and "My Journey" continue to motivate young minds.

Legacy and Remembrance:

Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong—doing what he loved most: teaching and inspiring youth. His sudden demise was a shock to the entire nation, but his ideas and ideals live on. He remains a symbol of what one can achieve with dedication, discipline, and a love for the nation. His life story is a message that no dream is too big if one is willing to work for it.
